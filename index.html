About David and the Studio

Welcome to my stained glass studio, nestled in the heart of the Pyrenees in southwest France, where I create unique stained glass pieces and teach others to embrace the beauty of this timeless craft. My name is David, and I’ve been working as a stained glass artist for over five years, specializing in both lead work and Tiffany-style techniques. Stained glass is not just an art form—it’s a medium through which I capture the magic of light and color. Whether I’m designing a custom piece or guiding students in my workshops, my focus is always on craftsmanship and creativity. I’m passionate about sharing this art with others and helping them discover the joy of creating their own stained glass masterpieces. Journey into Stained Glass My path to stained glass began during a break from work, a time when I was searching for something that could reconnect me with creativity. That’s when I discovered the enchanting world of stained glass. I was drawn to the way light transformed glass into vibrant art, and I knew I had found something special. I trained in France, learning from experienced artisans who passed down traditional techniques. Over time, I developed my own style, blending classic craftsmanship with modern flair. Today, my studio is based on an alpaca farm in the Pyrenees, where I create custom stained glass pieces and teach others to explore their own creative potential. Teaching quickly became an important part of my journey—I love seeing people discover the same passion for stained glass that first captured my heart. Craftsmanship and Custom Designs At the core of my work is a dedication to craftsmanship. Stained glass demands precision and patience, and I take pride in ensuring that every piece I create is made with the utmost care and attention to detail. Whether working with traditional lead came or the intricate Tiffany technique, I enjoy the challenge of designing pieces that are both beautiful and meaningful.

1 Hour training rate

Bespoke 35 euro

By the end of the training you will have completed from design to finish two pieces minimum. Firstly with lead and then a second piece using the Tiffany method. We may find time to make an additional Tiffany piece. Upon completion of the training you will be capable of designing and making your own work either at your home or back in my workshop under my supervision should you wish to remain engaged. I run a five day course from 9am through to 5pm with an hour or more for lunch. There will be tea making facilities and juice etc on hand. This training is a great opportunity for you to develop a new skill from which you can make and sell your work. Alternatively just make gifts and trinkets for family and friends, I will train you to ensure you are capable of both and you can recoup your course fee in no time…… look out next years Christmas market…….
